SIP Project Proposal

Group 2B

Introduction

Project Title Determining the Most Effective Rose Petal Compost for Increased Basil Yield
Problem Which type of rose petal compost can produce the most amount of basil leaves?
Hypothesis If we use in-between rose petals consisting of half alive and half-dried petals as our compost, then it will produce the most amount of basil leaves.

VARIABLES

Independent Variable In-between rose petals consisting of half alive and half-dried petals as our compost
Dependent Variable/s Amount of basil leaves produced
Constant or Controlled Variables
-Type of herb (basil)
-Type of soil used
-Amount of water used
-(Other controlled variables include the amount of rose petals for each setup, amount of sunlight exposure received by the herbs, amount of soil used, size of pot used for planting, and location of the planted herbs)

TREATMENTS / SET-UP

Control Set-up Experimental Set-up 1 Experimental Set-up 2 Experimental Set-up 3
The controlled set-up only consists of a basil herb with no addition of the independent variable. Experimental set-up 1 consists of a basil herb with the addition of live rose petals. Experimental set-up 2 consists of a basil herb with the addition of dried rose petals. Experimental set-up 3 consists of a basil herb with the addition of half-alive, half-dried rose petals.
3 Trials 3 Trials 3 Trials 3 Trials

Description of Measurement or Observation Significance of Observation
Number of green leaves of plant will be counted every 3 days One of the dependent variables; a measurement related to plant growth rate
Laboratory testing for %N of compost sample after a 2 months period of composting; 3 samples per composting set-up will be tested. Nitrogen is a plant nutrient that should be present in the soil/compost. It will help explain the effect of compost on plant growth.
